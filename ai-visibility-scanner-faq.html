AI Visibility & GEO — Website FAQ

A plain-English primer on AI visibility and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — what makes a website easy for AI assistants to discover, parse and cite, and how the AI Visibility Scanner fits in. This is general guidance, not a guarantee of any particular outcome.

What is "AI visibility"?

AI visibility is how easily AI assistants — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity and AI-powered search — can discover your website, understand what it says, and cite it when answering a user. It rests on the same technical foundations as classic SEO (clean HTML, a sitemap, structured data) plus a few AI-specific signals. The discipline of optimising for it is often called Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) or Answer Engine Optimization.

Is this the same as SEO?

It overlaps heavily but is not identical. Most of what helps search engines also helps AI assistants: a reachable page, a sitemap, a clear title and description, structured data and real content. AI visibility adds a few things on top — machine-readable structured data the model can lift directly (especially FAQ markup), clearly chunked content, and emerging conventions like llms.txt. Think of it as SEO's foundations plus AI-specific clarity.

What is llms.txt and do I need it?

llms.txt is a plain-text or Markdown file placed at your site root (like robots.txt) that points AI tools to your most important pages and content in a clean, easy-to-parse form. It was proposed in late 2024 and is being adopted by a growing number of sites, but it is an emerging community convention, not an official standard, and no AI provider is required to read it. The scanner weights it highly because it is the clearest AI-specific signal a site can add today — but its absence breaks no rule.

Why does structured data (schema.org) matter for AI?

Structured data (schema.org, usually as JSON-LD) describes your content in a format machines read unambiguously — who you are (Organization), where you are (LocalBusiness), and questions you answer (FAQPage). AI assistants and AI search can lift these facts directly instead of guessing them from prose, which makes your site easier to summarise and cite accurately. It is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-risk things most sites can add.

Why is FAQ schema weighted so heavily?

Because a question-and-answer pair is exactly the shape an AI assistant needs. FAQPage structured data hands the model clean, self-contained answers it can quote, which makes your content far more likely to be surfaced for the questions you cover. Pairing genuine, useful answers with FAQPage markup is one of the most direct ways to become citable — which is why it is among the heaviest checks.

Do robots.txt and the sitemap still matter for AI?

Yes. A robots.txt tells crawlers — including AI crawlers — what they may access, and an XML sitemap helps them find all your pages efficiently. If you want AI tools to read your site, make sure your robots.txt does not accidentally block their user-agents, and keep a current sitemap. These are basic, well-established signals; the scanner checks that they exist and are well-formed.

Can you guarantee ChatGPT or Perplexity will cite my site?

No, and be wary of any tool that claims to. Whether an assistant cites you depends on the user's exact question, the model, its training data and live-retrieval choices, your authority and competition — none of which a static scan can see or control. What this scanner does is measure the technical signals that make your site easier to discover, parse and cite. Improving them improves your odds; it does not promise an outcome.

What is the "AI Citation Probability" number?

It is an explicit heuristic, not a scientific prediction. It is derived from your deterministic score plus small bonuses for the signals AI assistants weigh most (FAQ schema, llms.txt, Organization markup). It is meant as an at-a-glance indicator of how complete your AI-visibility signals are — nothing more. We label it clearly as a heuristic precisely because no honest tool can predict real citation frequency.

Why do content depth and clear sections matter?

AI assistants cite content they can actually read and understand. A page with almost no text, or one whose meaning only appears after JavaScript runs, gives them little to work with. Substantial, well-structured copy — with recognisable Services, Pricing, About, FAQ and Contact sections — gives assistants clear, quotable material and helps them answer questions about you accurately.

Does a high score mean AI will recommend me?

No. A high score means your site is technically easy for AI to discover, parse and cite — the foundations are strong. It does not mean any assistant will recommend you, because that also depends on your reputation, reviews, the breadth and quality of your content, and competition, plus each provider's own systems. Treat the report as a prioritised to-do list for the parts you control, not a promise of visibility.
