        How it works

        The scanner fetches your URL and extracts real public signals from the server-rendered HTML — whether a privacy or cookie policy is linked, whether a consent banner or a known Consent Management Platform is present, which third-party tracking and marketing scripts load (Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, Hotjar and others), which embeds set cookies, and whether forms collect personal data. It then reviews them through a GDPR / ePrivacy lens. It reports only on what it can actually see; it does not guess. If the page can't be fetched, it tells you instead of inventing a score.

        What it checks

        • Privacy policy link
        • Cookie policy / notice
        • Consent banner / CMP
        • Analytics & tracking scripts
        • Advertising pixels
        • Cookie-setting embeds
        • Hotlinked Google Fonts
        • Form data-collection

        This is an indicative, signals-based pre-audit — not legal advice. It does not establish compliance, replace a Data Protection Officer, or constitute a legal or technical certification.

        The report is based on public page signals fetched from the URL you provide (server-rendered HTML only). It analyses static HTML, so it cannot verify whether trackers actually fire before consent, what cookies are really stored at runtime, the lawful basis, or the legal completeness of any policy. If the page cannot be fetched, no scored report is produced.
